Visualization 1: Discover prostitution against vehicle theft in SF (2013-2015)

Take a look at Prostitution vs Crime Theft in the area of San Francisco in the responsive scatterplot below. The main question we want to answer here is "How both crimes developed over time?"

Features:

  • Switch between years 2013 and 2015 to get an idea how crime developed in each district
  • The size of each circle reflects the total number of crimes committed in the selected district and year
  • Hover over circle to see which district it belongs to.

Visualization 2 (optional): Discover temporal development of crimes

This visualization aims at temporal patterns of crimes across 24h of the day. The main question we want to answer is "If we knew that a crime is about to happen in SF, at which time it happens the most likely?"

Features:

  • Each bar reflects the overall number of crimes committed at this hour
  • The exact number you find by hovering over the bar
  • Choose the focus category from the dropdown.

Visualization 3: Discover clustering of crimes using K-means

This visualization divides crimes, in particular Prostitution, into distinct geographical partitions. The main question we want to answer is "Are there geographical patterns to be discovered?" and "What are the key areas for prostitution in SF?"

Features:

  • Each big circle represents a centroid - the center of each partition of crimes
  • The whole map is divided into hexagons - areas containing crimes (if coloured)
  • Hexagons of the same color belong to the same partition
  • You can choose the number of partitions the dropdown (e.g., K = 2 groups)
  • Hover over circle to get the coordinates and the exact number of crimes in the partition.

Visualization 4 (optional): Discover distribution of crimes using Choropleth

This visualization aims at distribution of crimes across SF. The main question we want to answer is "If we knew that a crime is about to happen in SF, in which district it happens the most likely?"

Features:

  • Choropleth below shows the map of SF divided into districts
  • Color (see legend) of a district reflects the number of crimes committed there
  • The exact number you find by hovering over the district
  • You are also able to choose the focus category from the dropdown below.
